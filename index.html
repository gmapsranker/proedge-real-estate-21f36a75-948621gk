Frequently Asked Questions About Property Appraisal and Valuation

How much does a residential property appraisal cost in Angeles City?
Our residential property appraisal fees in Angeles City typically range from ₱8,000 to ₱15,000, depending on the property size and type. Single-family homes under 200 sqm usually cost ₱8,000-₱10,000, while larger properties or those with multiple structures may cost more. We provide a precise quote after understanding your property details. All fees include physical inspection, market analysis, and a comprehensive written report delivered within 5-7 business days.
Are your appraisers licensed to work in Pampanga? How can I verify this?
Yes, all our appraisers are licensed by the Professional Regulatory Board of Real Estate Service (PRBRES) and registered to work in Pampanga. Our lead appraiser holds PRC License No. 0123456 which you can verify through the PRC website. We also maintain active membership with the Philippine Association of Real Estate Appraisers (PARA) and strictly follow their professional standards. We'll gladly provide copies of our credentials upon request.
How long does the appraisal process take from inspection to final report?
For most Angeles City properties, our complete appraisal process takes 5-7 business days. Here's the typical timeline: Property inspection (1-2 hours), market data gathering and analysis (2-3 days), report preparation (2-3 days). Rush services are available for an additional fee, with reports possible within 3 business days. Note that complex properties or those in remote areas of Pampanga may require additional time.
Do you provide appraisals for bank loan purposes? Will banks accept your reports?
Yes, we regularly provide appraisals for bank loan purposes and our reports are accepted by major banks in Angeles City and throughout Pampanga, including BDO, BPI, Metrobank, and Security Bank. Our reports meet all BSP requirements and banking standards. We can also coordinate directly with your bank's credit department if needed. Just let us know which bank you're working with so we can ensure we meet their specific format requirements.
What information do I need to provide for an accurate appraisal of my property?
To ensure an accurate appraisal, please provide: 1) Copy of your property title or TCT, 2) Tax declaration, 3) Latest real property tax receipt, 4) Any building permits or plans if available, 5) Access to all areas of the property during inspection. For properties in gated subdivisions in Angeles City, please also arrange gate passes or subdivision access for our appraiser. If you're missing any documents, we can still proceed but having these ready helps ensure the most accurate valuation and speeds up the process.
